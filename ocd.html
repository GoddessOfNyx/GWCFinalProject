OBSESSIVE-COMPULSIVE DISORDER (OCD)

SIGNS/SYMPTOMS

  • Obsessions: repeated thoughts, urges, or mental images that cause anxiety
    • --> Common obsessions include fear of germs, aggressive thoughts towards themselves or others, having things in perfect order

  • Compulsions: repetitive behaviors that one feels the need to do in response to an obsessive thought
    • --> Common compulsions include excessive cleaning, ordering things in a particular way, repeatedly checking on things, compulsive counting

  • No control over thoughts and behaviors

  • Spends at least 1 hour a day on those thoughts or behaviors

  • Does not feel satisfied when performing the behaviors or rituals, but does feel short term relief from the anxiety caused by those thoughts

  • Some individuals suffer from a tic disorder
    • --> Motor tics are sudden, brief, repetitive movements such as eye blinking, shoulder shrugging, facial grimacing, and head and shoulder jerking
    --> Vocal tics include repetitive throat clearing, sniffing, or grunting sounds

Over time, symptoms can come and go, ease, or worsen

HOW TO HELP

  • Remember that OCD behaviors are symptoms, not character traits, so avoid making personal criticisms

  • Don’t reprimand people with OCD or order them to stop performing behaviors because they can not comply, and pressure to stop will only make the behaviors worse

  • Each individual suffering from OCD needs to overcome problems at their own pace, so be as kind and patient as possible.

  • Praise any successful attempts to resist OCD

  • Do not support one’s OCD rituals, but rather support them as a person
