BIPOLAR DISORDER

SIGNS/SYMPTOMS

Bipolar Disorder can be identified by different episodes, as well as different types. The basic difference between the two is that Bipolar 1 has more severe manic episodes and Bipolar 2 causes hypomania.

Manic episodes:

  • Feeling exceedingly happy and energetic.

  • Feeling impatient/irritated.

  • Lack of sleep.

  • Becoming easily distracted or a boost in one's focus.

  • The individual may involve themself in activites like gambling and overspending.

  • Feelings of control over everything.

  • This type of episode can last at minimum, a week long.

    • Depressive episodes:

  • Feeling exceeedingly sad and down.

  • Can occur more often and lasts longer than a manic episode.

  • Harder to diagnose.

  • Extreme mood swings.

  • Lack or interest and focus.

  • Secluding oneself.

  • Feelings of powerlessness and shame.

  • Lack of sleep.

  • Suicidal thoughts/feelings.

    • HOW TO HELP

    Anyone can get Bipolar disorder, and living/coping with it can be difficult, but not impossible. It's important to remember that every life has value and this does not define a person.

  • Remember that treatment can be very helpful, and is different for each individual.

  • Educate yourself: The more you know, the better you will be able to help.

  • Medication is commonly used to treat Bipolar disorder, and should be discussed with a doctor.

  • Psychotherapy(talk therapy), can be encouraging, and comforting.
