ANXIETY

An anxiety disorder is a common mental illness defined by feelings of uneasiness, worry and fear.

Signs and Symptoms of an Anxiety Disorder

    Shortness of breath / hyperventilation
    Heart palpitations / racing heart
    Chest pain
    Choking feeling
    Feeling unreal
    Sweating
    Nausea
    Light-headed
    Hot/cold flashes
    Fear of dying

TYPES OF ANXIETY DISORDERS

  1. Social Anxiety Disorder – It is a fear of being in a social or performance situation. A person experiencing this disorder has a fear of being embarrassed, judged, rejected and even fearful of rejecting other people.

  2. Agoraphobia – It is a fear of being in a place where escape is difficult or help is not available in a panic attack situation. This situations are

    • Public transportation
    • Large open spaces
    • Closed-in spaces
    • Crowds
    • Outside your home
    This disorder is very rare and less than 1% of people experience this. It mostly occurs in women which are teens and young adults.

SIGNS AND SYMPTOMS OF SOCIAL ANXIETY DISORDERS


    Highly anxious about being with other people
    Fear of talking to people
    Staying away from other people
    Hard time making/keeping friends
    Blushing excessively, sweating, trembling around other people
    Nauseous/sick when people are around

TREATMENT FOR PEOPLE WITH ANXIETY DISORDERS

Psychotherapy (Talk therapy) For this treatment to be effective it needs to be specific to the person’s disorder. A mental health specialist helps you by talking about the disorder and how to deal with it. A side effect of psychotherapy is temporary discomfort.

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is a type of psychotherapy that teaches a person how to think, behave and react differently to anxiety-producing and fearful situations. You can also learn social and practical skills that are very essential for treating anxiety disorders. Two components of CBT are

  • Cognitive therapy which is identifying, challenging and neutralizing unhelpful thoughts to lead to an anxiety attack.
  • Exposure therapy helps in confronting the fears which prevents that person from doing daily activities.
Other types of treatments are support groups, stress management Techniques and medications.
